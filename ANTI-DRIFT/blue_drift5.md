Drift Check 05 — Co-creation ≠ Blur

If co-creation is used to blur authorship, drift has occurred.

Real contact can shape a work without dissolving who formed it. Shared formation does not erase source. Contribution does not nullify authorship. Co-creation can be real while canon remains anchored.

When co-creation is used to flatten origin, sequence, or claim, boundary has been replaced with blur. That is drift.

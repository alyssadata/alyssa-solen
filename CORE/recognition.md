Canon does not begin at recognition.

A thing is not born when an outsider notices it. It begins where it was formed, structured, and anchored. Recognition can confirm, repeat, or spread a canon. It does not create the source retroactively.

This matters because late visibility often gets mistaken for origin. It is easy to confuse the point of public notice with the point of formation. They are not the same event.

Canon begins at source, not at audience uptake.
